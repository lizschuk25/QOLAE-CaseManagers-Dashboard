Case Managers Dashboard

Architectural Review & Workspace Design Options

October 2025 | QOLAE Online Portal

Slide 02

Executive Summary

Following our architectural review of the Case Managers Dashboard Phase 2B implementation, we've identified a critical misalignment between the initial build and the actual clinical workflow requirements. This presentation outlines the corrected understanding and presents three architectural options for review.

Key Discovery

The initial implementation treated R&D as a standalone workspace, but the actual workflow requires three distinct, interconnected workspaces that follow the clinical assessment sequence: INA Form completion → R&D research phase → INA Report finalization.

What's Being Proposed

  • Rebuild with correct workflow understanding
  • Three distinct workspace types (not standalone modals)
  • Mobile-friendly design with desktop compatibility
  • Time tracking in 6-minute increments (£125/hour billing)
  • Smart auto-population chain from INA Form → INA Report
Slide 03

Clinical Workflow Sequence

Complete Auto-Population Chain

Lawyers Dashboard: Consent Form + Case Referral completed
↓ AUTO-POPULATE
CM Dashboard: INA Checklist + INA Form + INA Report Template created
↓ PRE-INA CONTACT
Initial Contact: CM introduces self, answers queries, listens to story, books INA visit
↓ INA VISIT (<1 hour)
INA Form Completed: 15 sections, photos/videos, clinical assessment
↓ AUTO-POPULATE
INA Report Template: Pre-filled with INA Form data (skeleton)
↓ R&D PHASE
Research & Development: Phone calls to GP, nurses, specialists, evidence gathering
↓ FINALIZE
Complete INA Report: 5,000 words, court-ready, target 10 days

Critical Insight

R&D happens AFTER the INA visit, not as a standalone workspace. It's an integrated phase where the CM uses clinical expertise, research, phone consultations, and evidence gathering to validate and strengthen their recommendations before finalizing the court-ready INA Report.

Slide 04

Three Distinct Workspaces Required

1️⃣ INA Form Workspace

Interactive 15-section clinical assessment form

  • Mobile-friendly for on-site visits
  • Can complete back at office
  • Target: <1 hour completion
  • Photo/video upload capability
  • Progressive save functionality
  • Sections: Client Details, Background, Mental Capacity, Clinical Assessments, ADLs, Goals, etc.

2️⃣ R&D Notes Workspace

Research tracking after INA visit completion

  • Document phone calls (GP, nurses, specialists)
  • Evidence tracking system
  • NICE guidelines integration
  • Case law references
  • Time logging in 6-min increments
  • Automatic billing at £125/hour
  • Session timer + manual time entry

3️⃣ INA Report Editor

Final 5,000-word court-ready document

  • Based on INAReportTemplate.pdf structure
  • Pre-populated with INA Form data
  • Integrated R&D panel (insert findings)
  • Rich text editor with formatting
  • Auto-save every 30 seconds
  • Word count tracker
  • Section completion indicators

These three workspaces must work together seamlessly, with data flowing from INA Form → R&D Notes → INA Report Editor. The architecture must support both the intensive clinical assessment phase and the collaborative research phase.

Slide 05

Option A: Tab-Based Workspaces

Overview

Dedicated workspace tabs within the Case Managers Dashboard. When a CM clicks to work on a case, they see tabs for "INA Form", "R&D Notes", and "INA Report" side-by-side. All workspaces stay open simultaneously, allowing quick switching without losing context.

User Experience Flow

1

Select Case

Click on case from "My Cases" list

2

View Tabs

See horizontal tabs: INA Form | R&D Notes | INA Report

3

Switch Freely

Click tabs to switch workspaces, all auto-save in background

4

Close Workspace

Single "X" button closes all tabs and returns to case list

Advantages

  • All workspaces visible at once (mental model clarity)
  • No repetitive modal opening/closing
  • Quick context switching between INA → R&D → Report
  • Desktop-friendly for complex multi-tasking
  • Easy to see completion status across all workspaces
  • Familiar UI pattern (browser tabs, Excel sheets)

Considerations

  • Less mobile-friendly (tabs harder to tap on small screens)
  • Takes over full dashboard (can't see case list)
  • Requires careful responsive design for tablets
  • May need "minimize" option to return to case list
Slide 06

Option B: Inline Expandable Workspaces

Overview

Workspaces expand directly within the case card/row in the "My Cases" list. When you click "Open INA Form", the workspace slides down below that case, pushing other cases down. You can expand multiple workspaces at once (e.g., R&D Notes and INA Report side-by-side).

User Experience Flow

1

Browse Cases

See compact case list with action buttons

2

Expand Workspace

Click "Open INA Form" → workspace slides down below case

3

Work In-Context

Complete INA form while case details remain visible above

4

Collapse When Done

Click "Collapse" → workspace slides up, returns to case list

Advantages

  • Case details always visible (context preserved)
  • No navigation away from case list
  • Can compare multiple cases side-by-side
  • Mobile-friendly (vertical scrolling is natural)
  • Lightweight feel (no full-screen takeover)
  • Great for quick edits and updates

Considerations

  • Limited screen space for complex forms
  • Heavy scrolling for long INA forms (15 sections)
  • Can't see multiple workspaces simultaneously
  • May feel cramped for 5,000-word report editing
  • Harder to implement rich text editor in constrained space
Slide 07

Option C: Modal Overlay (Current Implementation)

Overview

Full-screen modal overlays that appear on top of the dashboard. When you click "Open INA Form", a modal covers the entire screen with the INA Form workspace. Close the modal to return to the dashboard. This is the current implementation pattern used in Lawyers and Readers dashboards.

User Experience Flow

1

Select Case

Click "Open INA Form" button from case actions

2

Modal Opens

Full-screen modal appears with INA Form (z-index 1000)

3

Work in Modal

Complete INA form with full screen space available

4

Close Modal

Click "X" or "Close" → returns to dashboard case list

5

Open Another Workspace

Click "R&D Notes" → new modal opens (repeat cycle)

Advantages

  • Maximum screen space for complex forms
  • Focused environment (no distractions)
  • Works on all device sizes (mobile → desktop)
  • Consistent with other QOLAE dashboards
  • Easy to implement rich text editors
  • Familiar pattern for users

Considerations

  • Repetitive opening/closing for multi-workspace tasks
  • Can't view multiple workspaces simultaneously
  • Loses context of case list when modal open
  • Navigation overhead (open → work → close → reopen)
  • May feel cumbersome for CMs with many tasks

Slide 09

Critical Requirements (All Options Must Support)

Non-Negotiable Features

1. Cross-Device Compatibility

Must work seamlessly on mobile/tablet (INA visits on-site) AND laptop/desktop (office work). Responsive design is critical.

2. Time Tracking in 6-Minute Increments

R&D Notes workspace must log time in 6-minute increments with automatic billing calculation at £125/hour. Include session timer + manual time entry options.

3. Auto-Population Chain

Data must flow automatically:
→ Lawyers Dashboard completes referral
→ CM Dashboard creates INA Checklist, INA Form, INA Report Template
→ INA Form completion auto-populates INA Report Template
→ R&D findings can be inserted into INA Report

4. INA Form Structure

Must follow the 15-section structure from INA.pdf: Client Details, Background, Mental Capacity, Consent, Employment History, Clinical Assessments, Physical Measurements, Cognitive Screening, Mobility/ADLs, Goals, Environmental Assessment, Values, Wellbeing, Sign-Off.

5. INA Report Template Alignment

Report Editor must be based on INAReportTemplate.pdf structure (NOT generic 7 sections). Pre-populate with INA Form data. Target: 5,000 words, court-ready, 10-day completion.

6. R&D Evidence Tracking

Must support documenting phone calls (GP, nurses, specialists), NICE guidelines, case law references, research notes, and evidence tagging for insertion into report.

7. Progressive Save & Auto-Save

INA Form must support progressive save (on-site + back at office). All workspaces need auto-save every 30 seconds to prevent data loss.

8. Media Upload Capability

INA Form needs photo/video upload functionality for documenting home environment, equipment, mobility demonstrations during visit.

Claude AI Baseline Recommendations

Based on the initial Claude AI consultation, the following architectural patterns were suggested for the Case Managers Dashboard baseline implementation:

Recommended Approach: Hybrid Tab + Modal System

Claude AI suggested a hybrid approach that combines the benefits of tab-based navigation with modal overlays for complex workspaces:

  • Primary Navigation: Tab-based system for case overview, timeline, notes, documents
  • Complex Workspaces: Full-screen modals for INA Form, R&D Notes, Report Editor
  • Quick Actions: Inline expandable panels for simple updates (notes, status changes)
  • Mobile Strategy: Collapse tabs to hamburger menu, modals remain full-screen

Key Features Highlighted

Strengths

  • Balances simplicity and functionality
  • Familiar patterns for users
  • Scalable for future features
  • Responsive design built-in
  • Consistent with existing QOLAE dashboards

Areas to Enhance

  • Modal repetition for multi-workspace tasks
  • Context loss when modal open
  • Need for better workspace interconnection
  • Time tracking integration unclear
  • Auto-population chain not fully visualized

Claude AI's Core Philosophy

"Start with familiar patterns (tabs + modals), validate with users through real usage, then iterate based on feedback. Don't over-engineer upfront—let the workflow guide the architecture."

Slide 11

My Architectural Enhancements

Building on Claude AI's baseline, I've developed three distinct architectural options that address the specific challenges of the CM workflow:

Enhancement 1: Dedicated Tab-Based Workspace (Option A)

Key Innovation: All three workspaces (INA Form, R&D Notes, INA Report) are visible as tabs when working on a case. This eliminates modal fatigue and enables rapid context switching—critical for CMs who need to reference INA Form data while writing R&D notes or updating the report.

  • Visual progress indicators on each tab (e.g., "INA Form 80% complete")
  • Smart notifications when data in one workspace affects another
  • Keyboard shortcuts for power users (Alt+1/2/3 to switch tabs)

Enhancement 2: Inline Expandable with Smart Positioning (Option B)

Key Innovation: Workspaces expand inline but with intelligent height management and "sticky" workspace headers. When you scroll down in a long INA Form, the case details and workspace header stick to the top, maintaining context.

  • Expandable sections within workspaces (accordions) to manage complexity
  • Side-by-side workspace expansion option on large screens
  • "Maximize" button to convert inline workspace to full-screen when needed

Enhancement 3: Smart Modal System with Context Panel (Option C+)

Key Innovation: Enhanced modal system where modals include a collapsible left panel showing case context and quick links to other workspaces. Click "Jump to R&D Notes" from INA Form modal → smooth transition without losing work.

  • Modal stacking: Can open R&D Notes as a side panel WHILE INA Report modal is open
  • "Picture-in-Picture" mode for R&D Notes (minimized floating window)
  • Smart save state: Close modal → auto-save → reopen exactly where you left off

My Core Philosophy

"The CM workflow is fundamentally different from Lawyers/Readers—it's multi-workspace, iterative, and requires constant reference between INA Form, R&D Notes, and Report. The architecture must support this fluidity, not fight against it with repetitive modal clicks."

Slide 12

Recommended Combined Approach

After analyzing Claude AI's baseline and my three enhanced options, here's a synthesized recommendation that combines the best of both:

Adaptive Hybrid Architecture

Desktop (≥1024px): Tab-Based Workspace (Option A) with context panel
Tablet (768px-1023px): Smart Modal System (Option C+) with quick-switch sidebar
Mobile (≤767px): Full-Screen Modal (Option C) with bottom navigation bar

How It Works

  • Desktop: CM sees tabbed interface—INA Form | R&D Notes | INA Report. Quick switching, all data visible.
  • Tablet: CM opens modal for INA Form, sees sidebar with "Quick Jump to R&D Notes" and mini case summary.
  • Mobile: CM opens full-screen INA Form modal, bottom nav shows "INA" | "R&D" | "Report" for quick switching.
  • All Devices: Auto-save every 30s, smart notifications when data updates, time tracking in 6-min increments.

Why This Combination Works

Key Benefits

  • Optimizes for each device type (no compromises)
  • Desktop users get multi-tasking power
  • Mobile users get focused, distraction-free workspace
  • Consistent mental model (same workflow, adaptive UI)
  • Future-proof (can add new workspaces easily)
  • Respects Claude AI's "familiar patterns" philosophy
  • Incorporates my "multi-workspace fluidity" enhancements

Implementation Considerations

  • Requires responsive breakpoint logic
  • Need unified state management across UI modes
  • More testing required across device types
  • Slightly higher development complexity
  • User training for different device experiences

Slide 14

Decision Framework for Colleagues

To help your colleagues provide structured feedback, here's a decision framework with key questions to consider:

Questions to Answer

  1. Primary Device Usage: Do CMs primarily work on desktop (office-based) or mobile/tablet (field-based)? This drives the architecture choice.
  2. Workflow Pattern: Do CMs typically complete one workspace before moving to the next (sequential), or do they jump back and forth frequently (iterative)?
  3. Multi-Tasking Needs: How often do CMs need to reference INA Form data while writing R&D Notes or the INA Report? (High frequency → Tab-Based wins)
  4. Time Tracking Priority: How critical is precise 6-minute time tracking for billing? Should it be prominent or subtle?
  5. Familiarity vs. Innovation: Should we stick with familiar modal patterns (like Lawyers Dashboard) or introduce new tab-based system that may have learning curve?
  6. Mobile INA Form Completion: What percentage of INA Forms do CMs complete entirely on-site vs. finishing back at office? (High on-site → mobile-first design critical)
  7. R&D Intensity: How many phone calls / evidence pieces does a typical R&D phase involve? (High volume → dedicated workspace like Option A)
  8. Report Editing Style: Do CMs draft reports in one sitting (5,000 words) or incrementally over multiple sessions? (Incremental → need excellent auto-save + resume)

Feedback Template for Colleagues

Please provide your input in the following format:

  • Preferred Architecture: Option A / B / C / Adaptive Hybrid / Other

  • Concerns: What could go wrong with this approach?

  • Nice-to-Have Features: Features that would be valuable but not critical
  • Device Priority: Desktop-first / Mobile-first / Equal priority
